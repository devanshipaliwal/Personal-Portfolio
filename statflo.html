Email and Calling Integration @ Statflo

As part of my internship as a UI/UX Intern at Statflo, I undertook the task to develop the flow, logic, and ultimately re-envision the messenger UI at Statflo to include new communication channels - Email & Calling.

My Role

Tools

Duration

3 Weeks (April 2021)




Project Aims

  • Develop built-in app functionality for users to conduct outreach to their clients through multi-channel communication.
  • Create a seamless flow to allow first outreach to be through either calling/emailing, AND allow for secondary outreach to be conducted through the same channels.

Why does this matter?

  • Currently, users can only conduct outreach through SMS, which is a big user pain point as these messages have a short character limit. As a result, the following cannot be sent to their clients; Contracts, offers, and long links.
  • Many users have requested calling functionality, as it is quicker and more efficient when engaged in a long discussion with their client.
  • Calling functionality will also allow users to continue conducting outreach and sending chat starters while on call with other clients

Limitations

Due to confidentiality reasons, the wireframes and usability testing results for this project cannot be shared (as they depict features that Statflo has not yet released).


Journey Mapping - Email

Mapping the user flow through a new communication channel helped us recognize and prioritize some key functionalities;

  • Ability to create free formed email messages, or use premade templates
  • View, expand, and reply to email threads
  • Sort messenger column by SMS, email, and calling



Journey Mapping - Calling

Key Takeaways of the flow, and important functionalities to consider;

  • Ability to conduct first outreach through call
  • Ability to record, use a keypad, take notes, end call
  • Navigate the UI while calling a client
  • Receive inbound calling



Email Flow and Logic

There are 2 main flows for email integration; email as initial outreach, and as secondary outreach.

The first 3 screens show initial outreach, specifically;

  • Availability to choose a chat template, compose a personal email, and see the email thread once it has been sent.

The last 3 screens show an email as secondary outreach, specifically;

  • Pre-initiated conversation with email in the messenger thread, composing a free formed email and replying to an email thread.

Call Flow and Logic

Similar to email communication, there are 2 main flows for call integration; call as initial outreach, and as secondary outreach.

The screens below show a calling fully integrated as secondary outreach, specifically;

  • Confirmation to call the selected client, the UI once the call has been placed, and how the messenger will behave if a user navigates to other areas of the app while on call.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathizing with users is key. If it's not made with the user in mind, it won't be well received.
  • Creating new flows and logic within a system can be difficult, but as designers we must stick with precedent and pre-established logic to guide new flows. Deviation from app logic will confuse users.
  • Extensive research and justification behind design decisions can prove fruitful for both users and designers.

Users gain an empathetic and understanding product designer who will keep their pain points in perspective. Designers gain justification for their decisions and validation that users will ultimately be more satisfied once using the product.
